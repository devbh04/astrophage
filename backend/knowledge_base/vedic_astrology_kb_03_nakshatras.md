# Vedic Astrology Knowledge Base — Part 3: The 27 Nakshatras

## 11. NAKSHATRA SYSTEM — OVERVIEW

Nakshatras (lunar mansions) are 27 equal divisions of the zodiac, each spanning 13°20′. The Moon transits one nakshatra approximately every day. The nakshatra of the Moon at birth is the **Janma Nakshatra** (birth star) — the single most important nakshatra for timing, compatibility, and prediction in Vedic astrology.

### 11.1 Nakshatra Fundamentals

**Total Nakshatras**: 27 (some systems use 28, adding Abhijit)  
**Span**: 13°20′ each (360° ÷ 27)  
**Padas**: Each nakshatra is divided into 4 quarters (padas) of 3°20′ each  
**Navamsha connection**: Each pada corresponds to one navamsha sign  

### 11.2 Nakshatra Qualities

**Gana (Temperament)**:
- **Deva (Divine/Godly)**: Hastha, Swati, Punarvasu, Pushya, Shatabhisha, Anuradha, Shravana, Revati, Mrigashira
- **Manushya (Human)**: Rohini, Ardra, Purva Phalguni, Uttara Phalguni, Purva Ashadha, Uttara Ashadha, Purva Bhadra, Uttara Bhadra, Bharani
- **Rakshasa (Demonic/Fierce)**: Ashwini, Krittika, Ashlesha, Magha, Chitra, Vishakha, Jyeshtha, Mula, Dhanishtha

**Nadi (Energy Channel — used in Kundali Milan)**:
- **Aadi Nadi (Vata/Wind)**: Ashwini, Ardra, Punarvasu, Uttara Phalguni, Hastha, Jyeshtha, Mula, Shatabhisha, Purva Bhadra
- **Madhya Nadi (Pitta/Fire)**: Bharani, Mrigashira, Pushya, Purva Phalguni, Chitra, Anuradha, Purva Ashadha, Dhanishtha, Uttara Bhadra
- **Antya Nadi (Kapha/Water)**: Krittika, Rohini, Ashlesha, Magha, Swati, Vishakha, Uttara Ashadha, Shravana, Revati

**Yoni (Sexual compatibility symbol)**: Each nakshatra has an animal symbol representing sexual/intimate nature. Used in Kundali Milan compatibility.

## 12. THE 27 NAKSHATRAS IN DETAIL

### 12.1 ASHWINI (अश्विनी)

**Number**: 1  
**Span**: 0°00′ – 13°20′ Aries  
**Ruler**: Ketu  
**Deity**: Ashwini Kumaras (twin divine physicians)  
**Symbol**: Horse's head  
**Gana**: Rakshasa (demon)  
**Guna**: Rajas-Tamas-Tamas  
**Yoni**: Horse (male)  
**Nadi**: Aadi (Vata)  
**Caste**: Vaishya  
**Color**: Blood red  
**Lucky Letters**: Chu, Che, Cho, La  
**Lucky Day**: Tuesday  
**Lucky Number**: 1  
**Ruling Body Part**: Knees, upper feet  
**Dasha Vimshottari Years**: Ketu rules → 7 years  

**Core Themes**: Speed, healing, pioneering, spontaneity, instinct, medicine, new beginnings.

**Characteristics**: Ashwini is the very first nakshatra, imbued with the energy of beginnings and speed. Like twin horses, Ashwini natives are quick, restless, and energetic. They are often gifted healers or doctors (the Ashwini Kumaras are physicians of the gods). They start things quickly, sometimes too quickly. There is a youthful, impetuous quality — the "just do it" energy. They dislike being slowed down. Hair, especially beautiful hair, is associated with Ashwini.

**On the Negative Side**: Impatient, impulsive, arrogant, resistant to advice, can be reckless, lacks follow-through.

**Career Associations**: Medicine, healing arts, sports, military, transportation, horses, surgery, emergency response, pioneering ventures.

**Ashwini in different house positions**: Moon here → quick-thinking, athletic, health-conscious native; sometimes difficulty sitting still; love of horses or speed-related activities.

**Famous natives (approximate)**: Adolf Hitler (Ashwini Moon — shows the dangerous Rakshasa gana extreme), many military leaders.

**Compatible Nakshatras**: Shatabhisha (natural partner — both Rahu/Ketu axis), Shravana  
**Incompatible Nakshatras**: Vishakha (strained relationship)

### 12.2 BHARANI (भरणी)

**Number**: 2  
**Span**: 13°20′ – 26°40′ Aries  
**Ruler**: Venus  
**Deity**: Yama (god of death and dharma)  
**Symbol**: Yoni (womb/female sex organ)  
**Gana**: Manushya (human)  
**Guna**: Rajas-Rajas-Rajas  
**Yoni**: Elephant (female)  
**Nadi**: Madhya (Pitta)  
**Caste**: Outcaste/Mleccha  
**Color**: Blood red  
**Lucky Letters**: Li, Lu, Le, Lo  
**Lucky Day**: Friday  
**Lucky Number**: 9  
**Ruling Body Part**: Head, genitalia  

**Core Themes**: Transformation through birth and death, sexuality, containment, patience, purification.

**Characteristics**: Bharani is associated with Yama, the god of death and righteous judgment. This nakshatra deals with the polarity between creation and destruction — the womb (creation) and the grave (transformation). Bharani natives carry tremendous energy within, like the seed waiting to burst. They are sensual, creative, deeply sexual, and carry a heavy karmic load. They can handle extreme experiences — both pain and pleasure — and are often drawn to taboo subjects. There is a capacity for suffering that transforms into strength.

**Key Theme — Restraint**: The symbol of the yoni (containing vessel) means Bharani natives often need to learn restraint — of impulses, of expression, of sexuality. The best of Bharani manages their intense energies with discipline (Yama's other quality — dharmic judgment).

**On the Negative Side**: Possessive, jealous, overindulgent, moralistic yet tempted by vices, difficulty with delays.

**Career Associations**: Midwifery, gynecology, funeral/death industry, occult, sexuality research, law enforcement, judgment roles, creative arts exploring dark themes.

### 12.3 KRITTIKA (कृत्तिका)

**Number**: 3  
**Span**: 26°40′ Aries – 10°00′ Taurus  
**Ruler**: Sun  
**Deity**: Agni (god of fire)  
**Symbol**: Flame, razor blade  
**Gana**: Rakshasa  
**Guna**: Rajas-Rajas-Tamas  
**Yoni**: Sheep/Goat (female)  
**Nadi**: Antya (Kapha)  
**Caste**: Brahmin  
**Color**: White  
**Lucky Letters**: Aa, Ee, U, Ae  

**Core Themes**: Purification, sharp penetrating intelligence, cutting away impurity, fire, motherhood, Pleiades.

**Characteristics**: Krittika is associated with the Pleiades (six sisters) and the fire god Agni. It is the nakshatra of the Sun's exaltation (though the exact exaltation degree is 10° Aries, in Bharani). Krittika natives have a razor-sharp quality — they cut to the truth. They are independent, proud, and have strong principles. There is a nurturing quality (the six mothers — Krittika stars — are said to have raised the war-god Kartikeya), combined with critical penetrating judgment. They digest and process well — both food and experiences.

**On the Negative Side**: Overly critical, aggressive, stubborn, combative, can be unnecessarily harsh, burns bridges.

**Career Associations**: Military, surgery, butchery (all that involves sharp instruments), cooking/fire, metallurgy, engineering, spiritual purification practices.

### 12.4 ROHINI (रोहिणी)

**Number**: 4  
**Span**: 10°00′ – 23°20′ Taurus  
**Ruler**: Moon  
**Deity**: Brahma (creator)  
**Symbol**: Ox cart, chariot  
**Gana**: Manushya (human)  
**Guna**: Rajas-Rajas-Sattva  
**Yoni**: Serpent (male)  
**Nadi**: Antya (Kapha)  
**Caste**: Shudra  
**Color**: Bright red/white  
**Lucky Letters**: O, Va, Vi, Vu  
**Lucky Day**: Monday  

**Core Themes**: Creation, fertility, beauty, agriculture, sensuality, material abundance, fixity.

**Characteristics**: Rohini is the Moon's own nakshatra and the Moon's exaltation falls within its degrees (3° Taurus). Rohini is arguably the most beautifully placed nakshatra. It is associated with Brahma the creator — the cosmic womb of creativity and fertility. The Moon's favorite wife (among the 27 nakshatra wives) was Rohini, hence the Moon lingers here longest.

Rohini natives are beautiful, attractive, artistic, and magnetic. They draw people to them effortlessly. They have excellent taste in aesthetics — beauty, food, luxury, fashion. They are nurturing and maternal but also possessive. The ox cart symbol speaks to steady, reliable movement — not rushing but building persistently.

**The Rohini Beauty**: Rohini is considered the most beautiful of all nakshatras. Those with Moon, Venus, or Ascendant in Rohini often have notably attractive appearances.

**On the Negative Side**: Extremely possessive and jealous, materialistic, stubborn (fixed sign), self-indulgent, tendency to obsess over beauty and comfort.

**Career Associations**: Arts, fashion, agriculture/farming, music, beauty industry, gardening, food industry, creative professions, luxury goods.

**Famous**: Lord Krishna's Moon is in Rohini (hence considered divine).

**Nadi**: Madhya (Pitta)

**Color**: Silver/White

**Core Themes**: Searching, questing, the eternal seeker, gentle, curious, sensuality (Soma's nectar).

**Characteristics**: The Deer's head nakshatra. Mrigashira is the eternal seeker — always searching for something, never quite satisfied with what is found. The deer looks around nervously, ever alert, ever seeking the next beautiful thing. These natives are curious, gentle, sensitive, and romantic. They roam in relationships and interests, always wondering if something better is around the corner.

**On the Negative Side**: Restless, inability to commit, fickle, nervous, anxiety-prone, may be naive or overly idealistic.

**Career Associations**: Research, travel, sales, beauty industry, hunting, gemology, spiritual seeking.

### 12.6 ARDRA (आर्द्रा)

**Number**: 6  
**Span**: 6°40′ – 20°00′ Gemini  
**Ruler**: Rahu  
**Deity**: Rudra (the storm god — fierce form of Shiva)  
**Symbol**: Teardrop, diamond, fresh young plant  
**Gana**: Manushya (human)  
**Guna**: Tamas-Tamas-Tamas  
**Yoni**: Dog (female)  
**Nadi**: Aadi (Vata)  
**Color**: Green  
**Lucky Letters**: Ku, Gha, Na, Chha  

**Core Themes**: Storm, transformation through pain, intellectual sharpness, the cyclone, renewal after destruction.

**Characteristics**: Ardra means "moist" or "fresh" — the tear after the storm, the green shoot after the rain. This is the nakshatra of Rudra, the howling storm. Ardra natives experience profound transformations through difficult circumstances — often there is great suffering that leads to even greater renewal. They have razor-sharp analytical minds and can be brilliant scientists, intellectuals, or technical wizards. But beneath the intellectual surface, there is deep emotional turbulence.

**On the Negative Side**: Destructive tendencies, emotional manipulation, cruelty at times, suffering that becomes identity, excessive intellectualization at the expense of feeling.

**Career Associations**: Research, IT/technology, physics, surgery, mathematics, meteorology, radical innovation.

### 12.7 PUNARVASU (पुनर्वसु)

**Number**: 7  
**Span**: 20°00′ Gemini – 3°20′ Cancer  
**Ruler**: Jupiter  
**Deity**: Aditi (mother of the gods, infinity)  
**Symbol**: Quiver of arrows, house  
**Gana**: Deva (divine)  
**Guna**: Rajas-Sattva-Sattva  
**Yoni**: Cat (female)  
**Nadi**: Aadi (Vata)  
**Color**: Lead/dull  
**Lucky Letters**: Kay, Ko, Ha, Hee  

**Core Themes**: Return, renewal, restoration, abundance after scarcity, the eternal optimist, the infinite mother.

**Characteristics**: Punarvasu means "return of the good" or "becoming good again." It is the nakshatra of the eternal optimist and the sage who returns to society after contemplation. Jupiter-ruled Punarvasu natives are philosophical, wise beyond their years, generous, and have a quality of renewal — they bounce back from adversity. Lord Rama was born with his ascendant in Punarvasu, which is why this nakshatra is associated with dharmic kingship and righteousness.

The symbol of the quiver suggests arrows (actions) ready to be launched repeatedly — the native has inexhaustible resources within. The house symbol speaks to the return home, the comfort of belonging.

**On the Negative Side**: Can be too content to stay in comfort zones, sometimes gullible or overly trusting, may preach too much, superficial contentment.

**Career Associations**: Spiritual teaching, philosophy, medicine (healing), publishing, real estate, welfare work.

### 12.8 PUSHYA (पुष्य)

**Number**: 8  
**Span**: 3°20′ – 16°40′ Cancer  
**Ruler**: Saturn  
**Deity**: Brihaspati (lord of prayer, Jupiter's divine form)  
**Symbol**: Cow's udder, lotus, circle, flower  
**Gana**: Deva (divine)  
**Guna**: Rajas-Tamas-Sattva  
**Yoni**: Sheep/Ram (male)  
**Nadi**: Madhya (Pitta)  
**Color**: Blackish-red  
**Lucky Letters**: Hu, He, Ho, Da  

**Core Themes**: Nourishment, care, excellence, abundance, dharmic service, feeding others.

**Characteristics**: Pushya is considered one of the most auspicious nakshatras. Its name means "to nourish" or "to flourish." Despite being ruled by Saturn (often considered a malefic), Pushya's deity Brihaspati (Jupiter) gives it a profoundly benefic quality. Pushya natives are natural nurturers — they feed, care for, and support others emotionally and physically. They have a quality of excellence in all they do, like the udder that produces pure nourishing milk.

**Pushya Nakshatra is considered most auspicious for**: Starting new ventures, marriage, purchasing property, business inauguration. Many choose Pushya tithi/nakshatra coincidences as muhurta.

**Jupiter in Pushya in Cancer**: This combines Jupiter's exaltation sign (Cancer) with the most auspicious nakshatra. This is an extraordinarily powerful placement.

**On the Negative Side**: Overly self-sacrificing, can be rigid, fanatical about right behavior, food issues (both overindulgence and control).

**Career Associations**: Nutrition, food industry, caregiving, nursing, spiritual leadership, education (especially primary), farming.

### 12.9 ASHLESHA (आश्लेषा)

**Number**: 9  
**Span**: 16°40′ – 30°00′ Cancer  
**Ruler**: Mercury  
**Deity**: Nagas (serpent deities)  
**Symbol**: Coiled serpent  
**Gana**: Rakshasa  
**Guna**: Sattva-Rajas-Tamas  
**Yoni**: Cat (male)  
**Nadi**: Antya (Kapha)  
**Color**: Blackish-red  
**Lucky Letters**: Di, Du, De, Do  

**Core Themes**: Serpent wisdom, kundalini, manipulation, deep intelligence, transformation through poison (or wisdom).

**Characteristics**: Ashlesha is the most psychologically complex nakshatra. Ruled by Mercury and governed by the Naga (serpent) deities, Ashlesha natives possess deep, penetrating intelligence, hypnotic charm, and a strategic, sometimes manipulative mind. Like the coiled serpent, they hold their energy until they strike. They can be extraordinary healers (serpent = medicine) or dangerous schemers (serpent = cunning).

Ashlesha rules the end of Cancer — the final degrees of the water sign, where emotion becomes overwhelming. Ashlesha Moon natives may struggle with deep-seated insecurities, possessiveness in relationships, and difficulty trusting others.

**Kundalini Connection**: Ashlesha is directly connected to the kundalini serpent energy in yogic tradition. Highly evolved Ashlesha natives may become powerful yogis or spiritual healers.

**On the Negative Side**: Manipulative, possessive, controlling, vindictive, can use charm maliciously, difficulty with trust.

**Career Associations**: Psychology, counseling, hypnotherapy, yoga (kundalini), intelligence agencies, politics, writing (especially psychological thrillers), serpent handlers/worshippers in traditional contexts.

### 12.10 MAGHA (मघा)

**Number**: 10  
**Span**: 0°00′ – 13°20′ Leo  
**Ruler**: Ketu  
**Deity**: Pitrs (ancestors)  
**Symbol**: Royal throne, palanquin  
**Gana**: Rakshasa  
**Guna**: Tamas-Rajas-Rajas  
**Yoni**: Rat (male)  
**Nadi**: Antya (Kapha)  
**Color**: Ivory/cream  
**Lucky Letters**: Ma, Mi, Mu, Me  

**Core Themes**: Ancestral honor, royalty, past-life legacy, authority, pride, tradition.

**Characteristics**: Magha means "mighty" or "magnificent." Its deity is the Pitrs — the ancestors — and this nakshatra is deeply connected to lineage, tradition, and the blessings of ancestors. Magha natives often carry the legacy of a notable family or caste. They have a natural dignity and regal bearing. The throne symbol speaks to authority earned through heritage.

The Ketu rulership creates a paradox: past-life wisdom (Ketu) in a nakshatra of royal worldly power (Leo). Magha natives may struggle between worldly ambition and spiritual detachment, or may achieve great worldly authority only to find it feels empty.

**Ancestor Worship**: Magha is considered especially important for performing Pitru Tarpan (ancestral offerings). If Magha is afflicted in a chart, the native may be encouraged to perform ancestral rites.

**On the Negative Side**: Arrogant, overly proud of lineage, lazy (relying on past glory), harsh, authoritarian.

**Career Associations**: Politics, royal/government administration, law, historical research, genealogy, temple/religious authority.

**Number**: 11  
**Span**: 13°20′ – 26°40′ Leo  
**Ruler**: Venus  
**Deity**: Bhaga (god of delight and marital happiness)  
**Symbol**: Front legs of a bed/couch, hammock, fig tree  
**Gana**: Manushya  
**Guna**: Tamas-Tamas-Rajas  
**Yoni**: Rat (female)  
**Nadi**: Madhya (Pitta)  
**Color**: Light brown  
**Lucky Letters**: Mo, Ta, Ti, Tu  

**Core Themes**: Pleasure, love, creative expression, resting, beauty, patronage of the arts.

**Characteristics**: Purva Phalguni is the nakshatra of Bhaga — the god who governs marital bliss, good fortune, and the pleasures of life. The symbol of the bed speaks to rest, pleasure, and intimacy. Venus-ruled in the Sun's sign, Purva Phalguni natives love beauty, art, music, dance, romance, and leisure. They have magnetic personalities and are often talented performers.

Lord Shiva married Parvati under this star, making it auspicious for marriages. Famous performer Marilyn Monroe had her Moon in Purva Phalguni.

**On the Negative Side**: Vain, indulgent, pleasure-seeking to excess, can be lazy, dependent on luxury, prone to affairs.

**Career Associations**: Entertainment, performing arts, fashion, luxury goods, romantic partnerships/marriage counseling, design.

**Number**: 12  
**Span**: 26°40′ Leo – 10°00′ Virgo  
**Ruler**: Sun  
**Deity**: Aryaman (god of patronage and friendship, contracts)  
**Symbol**: Back legs of a bed/couch, fig tree  
**Gana**: Manushya  
**Guna**: Tamas-Tamas-Sattva  
**Yoni**: Cow (male)  
**Nadi**: Madhya (Pitta)  
**Color**: Bright red  
**Lucky Letters**: Te, To, Pa, Pi  

**Core Themes**: Service, marriage (formal commitment), noble patronage, contracts, duty.

**Characteristics**: Where Purva Phalguni is the honeymoon, Uttara Phalguni is the marriage — the formal commitment to another. Governed by Aryaman (god of contracts and nuptials), this nakshatra represents the dharmic duty within relationships. Uttara Phalguni natives are generous patrons, helpful to others, and have a strong sense of duty. They are more service-oriented than their sister nakshatra. The Sun rulership gives them natural authority and warmth.

**On the Negative Side**: Stubborn about their ways, may feel underappreciated for their service, can be self-righteous.

**Career Associations**: Social work, government service, counseling, patronage of arts, marriage/family services.

### 12.13 HASTHA (हस्त)

**Number**: 13  
**Span**: 10°00′ – 23°20′ Virgo  
**Ruler**: Moon  
**Deity**: Savitar (the Sun as the creative, life-giving force)  
**Symbol**: Hand, palm of the hand  
**Gana**: Deva (divine)  
**Guna**: Rajas-Tamas-Sattva  
**Yoni**: Buffalo (female)  
**Nadi**: Aadi (Vata)  
**Color**: Green  
**Lucky Letters**: Pu, Sha, Na, Tha  

**Core Themes**: Skill with hands, craft, humor, shrewdness, practical intelligence, healing touch.

**Characteristics**: Hastha means "hand." This nakshatra governs all activities done with the hands — craftsmanship, healing (laying on of hands), writing, typing, massage, surgery, and artisanship. Moon-ruled in Mercury's sign of Virgo, Hastha natives are skilled, practical, and often humorous. They have a quality of lightness and dexterity, both physical and mental.

**The Hastha Humor**: Hastha natives often have a quick, clever sense of humor. They can be playful tricksters.

**On the Negative Side**: Cunning, mischievous, overly calculating, can use their hands skills for theft or manipulation.

**Career Associations**: Surgery, massage therapy, palmistry, craftsmanship, writing, music (instrumental), architecture (draftwork), magic/sleight of hand.

### 12.14 CHITRA (चित्रा)

**Number**: 14  
**Span**: 23°20′ Virgo – 6°40′ Libra  
**Ruler**: Mars  
**Deity**: Vishwakarma (divine architect of the universe)  
**Symbol**: Bright jewel, pearl  
**Gana**: Rakshasa  
**Guna**: Tamas-Rajas-Tamas  
**Yoni**: Tiger (female)  
**Nadi**: Madhya (Pitta)  
**Color**: Black  
**Lucky Letters**: Pe, Po, Ra, Ri  

**Core Themes**: Creation of beautiful forms, architecture, brilliance, the dazzling jewel, artistic mastery.

**Characteristics**: Chitra means "the bright one" or "the brilliant." Its deity Vishwakarma is the divine architect and craftsman who built Lanka, Dwarka, and the celestial cities. Chitra natives have extraordinary aesthetic sense and technical creative ability. They excel at creating beautiful things — architecture, jewellery, fashion, film, art. The pearl/jewel symbol speaks to something rare and precious brought to form through effort.

Mars-ruled Chitra has a sharp, precise quality to its creativity — not soft and flowing, but dynamic and structured. Chitra natives are attractive, sometimes strikingly so.

**On the Negative Side**: Obsessive about appearance, deceptive (chitra also means "illusion"), vain, argumentative.

**Career Associations**: Architecture, interior design, fashion, jewelry, film-making, visual arts, engineering aesthetics, graphic design.

**Number**: 15  
**Span**: 6°40′ – 20°00′ Libra  
**Ruler**: Rahu  
**Deity**: Vayu (wind god)  
**Symbol**: Young plant shoot in the wind, sword, coral  
**Gana**: Deva (divine)  
**Guna**: Tamas-Tamas-Tamas  
**Yoni**: Buffalo (male)  
**Nadi**: Aadi (Vata)  
**Color**: Black  
**Lucky Letters**: Ru, Re, Ro, Ta  

**Core Themes**: Independence, flexibility (like a plant bending in wind), commerce, wandering.

**Characteristics**: Swati is the nakshatra of independence and adaptability. Like the young plant that bends without breaking in the wind, Swati natives are flexible, diplomatic, and resilient. Rahu-ruled, they are ambitious and somewhat unconventional. Venus's sign (Libra) gives them charm and social grace. They excel in business and trade (Vayu as wind = carrying commerce). They need freedom above all.

**On the Negative Side**: Indecisive (bends with every wind), changeable, sometimes dishonest in business dealings, difficulty committing.

**Career Associations**: Business/trade, aviation, communication, diplomacy, teaching, flexibility-requiring professions.

### 12.16 VISHAKHA (विशाखा)

**Number**: 16  
**Span**: 20°00′ Libra – 3°20′ Scorpio  
**Ruler**: Jupiter  
**Deity**: Indra and Agni (king of gods and fire)  
**Symbol**: Triumphal archway, potter's wheel  
**Gana**: Rakshasa  
**Guna**: Rajas-Rajas-Rajas  
**Yoni**: Tiger (male)  
**Nadi**: Aadi (Vata)  
**Color**: Golden  
**Lucky Letters**: Ti, Tu, Te, To  

**Core Themes**: Achievement of goals, ambition, perseverance, fierce determination, the champion.

**Characteristics**: Vishakha means "forked" or "two-branched." The triumphal arch symbolizes victory. Vishakha natives are intensely ambitious and have the perseverance of a potter shaping clay — patient effort yielding a beautiful result. Jupiter-ruled, they are philosophical but also intensely competitive. They set a goal and pursue it relentlessly. There is sometimes conflict between two major life goals or paths (forked symbol).

**The Indra-Agni Combination**: The king of gods (power, authority) plus fire (purification, action) → Vishakha natives want both worldly power and spiritual depth. This can create internal tension.

**On the Negative Side**: Aggressive, fanatical, jealous, manipulative when pursuing goals, can trample others.

**Career Associations**: Politics, sports championship, military, religious leadership, any field where single-pointed goal achievement is required.

### 12.17 ANURADHA (अनुराधा)

**Number**: 17  
**Span**: 3°20′ – 16°40′ Scorpio  
**Ruler**: Saturn  
**Deity**: Mitra (god of friendship, cosmic contracts)  
**Symbol**: Lotus flower, a row of offerings  
**Gana**: Deva (divine)  
**Guna**: Tamas-Sattva-Tamas  
**Yoni**: Hare (female)  
**Nadi**: Madhya (Pitta)  
**Color**: Reddish brown  
**Lucky Letters**: Na, Ni, Nu, Ne  

**Core Themes**: Friendship, devotion, loyalty, the lotus blooming in muddy water (spiritual ascent through difficulty).

**Characteristics**: Anuradha means "following Radha" or "another Radha." It follows Vishakha as success follows effort. Mitra (the god of contracts and friendship) governs this nakshatra, giving it qualities of deep loyalty, friendship, and cosmic harmony. Anuradha natives are deeply devoted to their loved ones and have a gift for maintaining friendships across time and distance. Like the lotus in Scorpio's murky water, they rise to beauty through difficult circumstances.

Saturn-ruled in Scorpio → discipline and transformation. These natives often develop profound spiritual discipline.

**On the Negative Side**: Can be overly self-sacrificing for friends, trouble with authority, tendency toward depression if isolated.

**Career Associations**: Social work, friendship/community building, occult research, travel, spiritual communities, psychology.

The amulet symbol speaks to protection through hidden knowledge.

**On the Negative Side**: Arrogant about seniority, domineering, can be jealous of others' success, may manipulate others "for their own good."

**Career Associations**: Military leadership, administration, occult, psychology, protective services, senior management.

### 12.19 MULA (मूल)

**Number**: 19  
**Span**: 0°00′ – 13°20′ Sagittarius  
**Ruler**: Ketu

### 13.3 Calculating Dasha Balance at Birth

The balance of the first dasha is proportional to how much of the birth nakshatra remains:

`Balance = (Total nakshatra duration) × (Remaining degrees in nakshatra ÷ 13.333°)`

Example: Moon at 5° in Ashwini (Ketu's nakshatra). Ashwini spans 0°–13°20′ Aries. 
- Moon has traversed 5° out of 13.333°
- Remaining: 8.333°
- Ketu's full dasha = 7 years
- Balance = 7 × (8.333 ÷ 13.333) = 7 × 0.625 = 4.375 years remaining in Ketu dasha at birth

# Vedic Astrology Knowledge Base — Part 2: Houses, Aspects & Yogas

## 6. THE TWELVE HOUSES (BHAVAS)

### 6.1 House Classification

**Kendra (Angular) Houses** — 1, 4, 7, 10: Strongest houses. Pillars of the chart. Planets here are most powerful.  
**Trikona (Trine) Houses** — 1, 5, 9: Dharmic houses. Most auspicious. Represent fortune and blessing.  
**Dusthana (Difficult) Houses** — 6, 8, 12: Houses of challenge, but also transformation and liberation.  
**Upachaya Houses** — 3, 6, 10, 11: Improve with time. Malefics here often ultimately help the native grow.  
**Maraka (Death-inflicting) Houses** — 2, 7: Their lords can trigger death or health crises during dashas.  
**Moksha Houses** — 4, 8, 12: Spiritual liberation, hidden dimensions.  
**Artha Houses** — 2, 6, 10: Material acquisition and worldly success.  
**Kama Houses** — 3, 7, 11: Desires, relationships, fulfillment.  
**Dharma Houses** — 1, 5, 9: Purpose, righteousness, fortune.

### 6.2 FIRST HOUSE (Pratham Bhava) — Lagna

**Sanskrit**: Lagna, Tanu Bhava  
**Significations**: Self, body, personality, appearance, character, beginning of life, health, overall vitality, early childhood, fame, head/brain  
**House Lord**: The lord of the rising sign  

**The Lagna is the most important point in the chart.** The Lagna lord's placement determines the chart's overall tone. A strong Lagna lord in a kendra or trikona = generally good life. Lagna lord in dusthana = challenges to health/self.

**Natural Karaka (Significator)**: Sun (for self/vitality)  

**Interpretation by Ascendant Sign**:
- Aries Lagna: Mars-ruled. Energetic, impulsive, athletic, competitive, independent
- Taurus Lagna: Venus-ruled. Grounded, artistic, comfort-loving, steady, sometimes stubborn  
- Gemini Lagna: Mercury-ruled. Intellectual, communicative, versatile, youthful, curious
- Cancer Lagna: Moon-ruled. Intuitive, nurturing, emotional, home-loving, psychic
- Leo Lagna: Sun-ruled. Dignified, generous, proud, leadership-oriented, dramatic
- Virgo Lagna: Mercury-ruled. Analytical, service-oriented, health-conscious, perfectionist
- Libra Lagna: Venus-ruled. Balanced, harmonious, relationship-oriented, aesthetic, diplomatic
- Scorpio Lagna: Mars-ruled. Intense, investigative, transformative, magnetic, secretive
- Sagittarius Lagna: Jupiter-ruled. Philosophical, optimistic, freedom-loving, teaching, travel
- Capricorn Lagna: Saturn-ruled. Disciplined, ambitious, cautious, responsible, improves with age
- Aquarius Lagna: Saturn-ruled. Humanitarian, unconventional, intellectual, progressive, friendly
- Pisces Lagna: Jupiter-ruled. Compassionate, spiritual, creative, dreamy, empathetic

### 6.3 SECOND HOUSE (Dwitiya Bhava) — Dhana Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Dhana Bhava (House of Wealth)  
**Significations**: Wealth, savings, family, speech, food, face, right eye, early education, accumulated resources, values, movable assets, voice quality, family lineage  
**Natural Karaka**: Jupiter (wealth), Moon (food/family)  
**Maraka house**: 2nd lord as maraka can trigger health crises  

**Key principles**:
- Benefics in 2nd → eloquent, wealthy, comes from good family
- Malefics in 2nd → speech issues, family conflicts, money challenges
- 2nd lord in kendra/trikona → wealth and good family
- Multiple planets in 2nd → many sources of income (if benefic) or complexity around wealth (if malefic)

**Planets in the 2nd House**:
- Sun: Government income, authoritative speech, family pride, father's wealth, eye issues possible
- Moon: Fluctuating wealth, emotional relationship with money, nourishing speech, good for food business
- Mars: Heated/direct speech, may damage wealth through impulsive spending or earn through real estate
- Mercury: Wealth through business/communication, clever with money, articulate, literary abilities
- Jupiter: Abundant wealth, eloquent and wise speech, generous family, excellent 2nd house placement
- Venus: Beauty-related income, melodious voice, artistic family, luxurious lifestyle aspirations
- Saturn: Slow wealth accumulation, may struggle with family, disciplined saver but delayed gains
- Rahu: Unconventional income, foreign sources of money, speech may be unusual or amplified
- Ketu: Detachment from wealth, may earn through spirituality, speech is often brief/mystical

### 6.4 THIRD HOUSE (Tritiya Bhava) — Parakrama Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Parakrama Bhava (House of Valor/Effort)  
**Significations**: Courage, siblings (especially younger), communication, writing, short journeys, hands/arms/shoulders, neighbors, skills, hobbies, media, social media, self-effort  
**Natural Karaka**: Mars (courage), Mercury (communication)  

**Key principles**:
- Malefics here (Mars, Saturn, Rahu) are actually good — Upachaya house, they give courage, self-made success
- Strong 3rd → successful communication career, brave, skilled with hands
- Afflicted 3rd → troubled relations with siblings, communication difficulties

---

### 6.5 FOURTH HOUSE (Chaturthi Bhava) — Sukha Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Sukha Bhava (House of Happiness/Comfort)  
**Significations**: Mother, home, property, vehicles, education (primary), heart, emotional foundation, homeland/birthplace, inner happiness, chest area  
**Natural Karaka**: Moon (mother/emotions), Mars (property/vehicles), Mercury (education)  

**Key principles**:
- Strong 4th → happy home life, good relationship with mother, property ownership, vehicles
- Afflicted 4th (especially by Saturn or Ketu) → separation from mother, troubled home, lack of inner peace
- Jupiter in 4th → very happy home, wise/spiritual mother
- Rahu in 4th → foreign home, unconventional relationship with mother or homeland

---

### 6.6 FIFTH HOUSE (Panchama Bhava) — Putra Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Putra Bhava (House of Children/Intelligence)  
**Significations**: Children (especially first child), intelligence, creativity, education (higher), romance, speculation/investment, past-life credit, mantras, spiritual practices, sports, entertainment  
**Natural Karaka**: Jupiter (children/wisdom), Venus (romance)  

**Key principles**:
- Strong 5th → intelligent children, creative genius, lucky in speculation, good romantic life
- Afflicted 5th → delays/difficulties in conception, troubled romance, poor speculative choices
- Sun in 5th → creative, talented children, government service through intelligence
- Moon in 5th → nurturing parent, emotional creativity, children are emotionally significant
- Jupiter in 5th → wise children, excellent for education and spirituality, Mantra siddhi possible

**5th House and Past Life**: The 5th house shows accumulated karma (Poorvapunya) — blessings or challenges from previous incarnations. A strong 5th = significant positive karma; an afflicted 5th = past karmic debts showing up in this life.

---

### 6.7 SIXTH HOUSE (Shashthi Bhava) — Shatru/Roga Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Ari Bhava (House of Enemies) / Roga Bhava (House of Disease)  
**Significations**: Enemies, obstacles, disease, debts, disputes, litigation, service, daily work, pets, healing, competition, maternal uncle  
**Natural Karaka**: Mars (enemies/competition), Saturn (chronic disease)  

**Key principles**:
- Upachaya house → malefics here improve over time, give competitive victory
- Strong 6th/6th lord → defeat of enemies, good health through discipline, service profession
- Planets in 6th are generally challenged but can show service professions (doctors, lawyers, military)
- 6th, 8th, 12th lords in kendra/trikona = Viparita Raja Yoga (reversal fortune yoga) — very powerful if pure

---

### 6.8 SEVENTH HOUSE (Saptama Bhava) — Kalatra Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Kalatra Bhava / Jaya Bhava (House of Spouse)  
**Significations**: Marriage, spouse/partner, business partnerships, public, open enemies, foreign travel, lower abdomen/urinary tract  
**Natural Karaka**: Venus (spouse for men), Jupiter (spouse for women)  
**Maraka house**: 7th lord is a natural maraka (death-inflicting) — can trigger end of life in its dasha in old age  

**Key principles**:
- Strong 7th → excellent marriage, good business partnerships, popular with public
- Afflicted 7th (especially by Mars or Saturn) → marital difficulties, delay in marriage
- Multiple planets in 7th → multiple relationships or complex marriage dynamics
- Saturn in 7th → delayed marriage (after 30), serious/older spouse, but lasting once committed
- Venus in 7th → beautiful/artistic spouse, harmonious marriage
- Mars in 7th → Mangal Dosha (especially for female charts), passionate but potentially combative marriage
- Rahu in 7th → foreign/unconventional spouse, non-traditional marriage

---

### 6.9 EIGHTH HOUSE (Ashtama Bhava) — Mrityu Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Mrityu Bhava (House of Death/Transformation)  
**Significations**: Death, longevity, transformation, occult/hidden knowledge, inheritance, sudden events, research, secrets, sex (psychological/transformative), chronic disease, mystical experiences  
**Natural Karaka**: Saturn (longevity/death), Mars (accidents/surgery)  

**Key principles**:
- Dusthana house — generally challenging for planets placed here
- 8th lord well placed → longevity, interest in hidden subjects, possible inheritance
- Benefics in 8th → interest in occult, some protection from sudden crises, but can indicate "comfortable death"
- Malefics in 8th → accidents, sudden crises, but also deep transformation and resilience
- Sun in 8th → father's health/longevity issues, inheritance possible, governmental secrets
- Moon in 8th → emotional turbulence, psychological depth, mother may face difficulties
- Jupiter in 8th → protection from death, esoteric wisdom, inheritance of knowledge

---

### 6.10 NINTH HOUSE (Navama Bhava) — Dharma Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Dharma Bhava / Bhagya Bhava (House of Fortune/Dharma)  
**Significations**: Father, fortune, dharma, religion, philosophy, higher education, long journeys, foreign travel, teacher/guru, law, spiritual practices, past karma from previous life  
**Natural Karaka**: Jupiter (dharma/wisdom), Sun (father)  

**Key principles**:
- Most auspicious trikona house after the 1st
- Strong 9th → extremely fortunate life, excellent relationship with father, naturally righteous, higher education, spiritual gifts
- Jupiter in 9th → most auspicious placement; Hamsa Yoga if in Sagittarius or Pisces
- Sun in 9th → devoted to dharma, government/authority connection, fortunate father
- Saturn in 9th → strict/disciplined father, fortune through hard work, philosophical pessimism then eventual wisdom

---

### 6.11 TENTH HOUSE (Dashama Bhava) — Karma Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Karma Bhava (House of Action/Career)  
**Significations**: Career, profession, status, reputation, authority, government, actions, karma, public life, employer, dharmic work  
**Natural Karaka**: Sun (authority), Saturn (labor/discipline), Mercury (business), Jupiter (teaching)  

**Key principles**:
- The house of worldly achievement
- Planets here are highly visible — directly affect reputation and career
- Sun in 10th → natural authority, government career, high status, father connection to career
- Saturn in 10th → slow career rise but eventual mastery, Shasha Yoga (Pancha Mahapurusha) if in own sign/exaltation
- Jupiter in 10th → wisdom-based career (teaching, law, spirituality), respected profession
- Multiple benefics in 10th → Raja Yoga potential, multiple career successes

---

### 6.12 ELEVENTH HOUSE (Ekadasha Bhava) — Labha Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Labha Bhava (House of Gains)  
**Significations**: Income, gains, elder siblings, social circles, hopes, wishes, left ear, large organizations, networks, friends, fulfillment of desires  
**Natural Karaka**: Jupiter (gains)  

**Key principles**:
- Upachaya house → malefics here grow stronger and ultimately help
- Strong 11th → substantial income, powerful social network, wishes fulfilled, elder siblings helpful
- Saturn in 11th → slow but steady gains, elder siblings may be absent or burdensome initially
- Rahu in 11th → large and unconventional gains, massive networks, foreign connections for income

---

### 6.13 TWELFTH HOUSE (Dwadasha Bhava) — Vyaya Bhava

**Sanskrit**: Vyaya Bhava (House of Expenditure/Loss)  
**Significations**: Expenses, losses, foreign travel/residence, isolation, hospitals, ashrams, prisons, spirituality, moksha, sleep, secret enemies, feet, left eye  
**Natural Karaka**: Saturn, Ketu (for spirituality/moksha)  

**Key principles**:
- Dusthana house — challenges, but profound spiritual significance
- Well-placed 12th lord → foreign settlement, spiritual attainment, peaceful sleep
- Ketu in 12th → excellent for moksha, detachment, spiritual liberation
- Venus in 12th → pleasures in secret, bed pleasures, artistry in private life, expenses on luxury
- Jupiter in 12th → spiritual wisdom, guru in foreign land, expenditure on noble causes

## 7. PLANETARY ASPECTS (DRISHTI)

### 7.1 Overview of Vedic Aspects

In Vedic astrology, aspects are calculated from sign to sign (whole-sign aspects), not by exact degree. Every planet aspects the 7th house from its position (opposition). Additionally, some planets have special aspects:

**Universal Aspect**: All planets aspect the 7th house from their position (full 100% aspect).

**Special Aspects**:
- **Mars** additionally aspects: 4th and 8th houses from its position
- **Jupiter** additionally aspects: 5th and 9th houses from its position  
- **Saturn** additionally aspects: 3rd and 10th houses from its position
- **Rahu/Ketu** additionally aspect: 5th/9th (some authorities: same as Jupiter)

### 7.2 Aspect Strength

| Aspect | Strength |
|--------|----------|
| 7th house | 100% (Full) |
| Special aspects (Mars 4th/8th, Jupiter 5th/9th, Saturn 3rd/10th) | 100% (Full) |
| All other houses | No aspect |

### 7.3 Interpreting Aspects

**Jupiter aspecting a house/planet**: Protects, expands, bestows wisdom and grace. Even malefic planets under Jupiter's aspect become more benign. Jupiter's aspect on the Lagna is highly protective.

**Saturn aspecting a house/planet**: Delays, restricts, disciplines, tests, and ultimately teaches. Saturn's aspect creates lessons and karmic weight. Saturn aspecting the Moon causes depression or serious mental challenges if Moon is weak.

**Mars aspecting a house/planet**: Energizes, heats, creates conflict or courage. Mars aspecting 7th (from 1st house position) = direct aspect on marriage house.

**Rahu aspecting a house/planet**: Amplifies, distorts, obsesses. Creates unusual or foreign-flavored results.

## 8. MAJOR YOGAS (PLANETARY COMBINATIONS)

### 8.1 Raja Yogas (Combinations for Power/Success)

**Definition**: A Raja Yoga forms when lords of kendra houses (1, 4, 7, 10) and trikona houses (1, 5, 9) conjoin, mutually aspect, or exchange signs. The more combinations, the more powerful.

**Key Raja Yogas**:

**Dhana Yoga** (Wealth Yoga): 
- Lords of 2nd and 11th conjoin or exchange = great wealth
- Lagna lord + 5th lord in 11th = excellent earnings through intelligence
- Lords of 1st, 2nd, 5th, 9th, 11th connections = financial prosperity

**Pancha Mahapurusha Yogas** (Five Great Person Yogas):
These occur when a planet is in its own sign or exaltation sign AND placed in a kendra (1, 4, 7, 10).

1. **Ruchaka Yoga** (Mars in own/exalted in kendra): Heroic, courageous, athletic, military prowess, aggressive leadership. Mars in Aries or Scorpio or Capricorn in 1st, 4th, 7th, or 10th.

2. **Bhadra Yoga** (Mercury in own/exalted in kendra): Highly intelligent, excellent communicator, business acumen, learned, witty. Mercury in Gemini or Virgo in kendra.

3. **Hamsa Yoga** (Jupiter in own/exalted in kendra): Wise, just, religious, learned, noble, fortunate, guru-like. Jupiter in Sagittarius, Pisces, or Cancer in kendra.

4. **Malavya Yoga** (Venus in own/exalted in kendra): Beautiful, artistic, romantic, wealthy, comfortable life, luxury, vehicles. Venus in Taurus, Libra, or Pisces in kendra.

5. **Shasha Yoga** (Saturn in own/exalted in kendra): Disciplined, authority in administration, powerful in service, leader of the masses, long-lived. Saturn in Capricorn, Aquarius, or Libra in kendra.

**Neecha Bhanga Raja Yoga** (Cancellation of Debilitation):
When a debilitated planet's debilitation is cancelled, it creates powerful results. Cancellation conditions:
- The lord of the sign the debilitated planet occupies is in a kendra from Lagna or Moon
- The planet that is exalted in the same sign as the debilitated planet is in a kendra from Lagna or Moon
- The debilitated planet is conjunct its exaltation lord
- The debilitated planet receives aspect from its exaltation lord

Example: Sun in Libra (debilitated at 10°). Sun's exaltation lord = Mars. If Mars is in a kendra from Lagna → Neecha Bhanga for Sun → powerful Neecha Bhanga Raja Yoga.

---

### 8.2 Dhana Yogas (Wealth Yogas)

1. **Lakshmi Yoga**: Lord of 9th is powerfully placed (own/exalt sign) and Lagna lord is also strong → great wealth, beauty, prosperity.

2. **Kubera Yoga**: Multiple planets in 11th house, especially benefics → multiple income streams, great financial gains.

3. **Vasumathi Yoga**: Benefics in upachaya houses (3rd, 6th, 10th, 11th) from Lagna or Moon → steady wealth accumulation.

4. **Chandra-Mangal Yoga**: Moon and Mars conjunct → entrepreneurial spirit, financial gains through property or real estate, determined pursuit of money.

---

### 8.3 Viparita Raja Yoga (Reversal/Paradox Yoga)

When lords of dusthana houses (6, 8, 12) occupy other dusthana houses (6, 8, or 12), the native rises through destruction of obstacles or enemies. Very powerful when the involved lords are not connected to kendras/trikonas.

- 6th lord in 8th or 12th
- 8th lord in 6th or 12th  
- 12th lord in 6th or 8th

**Three types**:
- **Harsha Yoga**: 6th lord in 8th or 12th → excellent health, defeat of enemies, success in competitive fields
- **Sarala Yoga**: 8th lord in 6th or 12th → longevity, fearlessness, possible wealth through transformation/inheritance
- **Vimala Yoga**: 12th lord in 6th or 8th → virtuousness, minimal expenses, spiritual liberation at end of life

---

### 8.4 Gaja Kesari Yoga

**Formation**: Jupiter in kendra (1st, 4th, 7th, or 10th) from Moon.  
**Results**: Wisdom, fame, recognition, good character, prosperity, long life, honored like a great person. One of the most commonly cited yogas.

**Strength depends on**:
- Jupiter's overall strength (sign, aspects, nakshatra)
- Moon's overall strength (phase, sign, aspects)
- Whether they both occupy dignified positions

---

### 8.5 Budha-Aditya Yoga

**Formation**: Sun and Mercury conjunct in the same sign.  
**Results**: Intelligence, education, government service, recognition, administrative capability.

**Note**: Mercury is very frequently with the Sun (rarely more than 28° away), so this yoga is common. Its power depends on the sign, whether Mercury is combust, and other aspects. Most powerful when in Leo (Sun's sign), Virgo (Mercury's exaltation), or Gemini/Virgo (Mercury's own signs).

---

### 8.6 Chandra Yoga Combinations

**Sunapha Yoga**: Planet(s) in 2nd from Moon (except Sun) → self-made wealth, initiative, strong personality.

**Anapha Yoga**: Planet(s) in 12th from Moon (except Sun) → well-known, comfortable, independent, detached from worldly desires.

**Durudhara Yoga**: Planets on both sides of Moon (2nd and 12th) → ambitious, determined, wealthy, social prominence.

**Kemadruma Yoga**: No planets in 2nd or 12th from Moon AND Moon alone in its sign → isolation, poverty, hardship (especially if Moon has no other support). One of the few negative yogas with real impact. Cancelled if Moon is in a kendra from Lagna or Jupiter, or if Moon is aspected by/with benefics.

---

### 8.7 Nabhasa Yogas (Sky Yogas) — Pattern Yogas

Based on the distribution pattern of planets across the chart:

**Rajju Yoga**: All planets in moveable signs (Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn) → constant traveling, wandering, restless nature.

**Musala Yoga**: All planets in fixed signs (Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius) → stable, unchanging, determined, stubborn nature.

**Nala Yoga**: All planets in dual signs (Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius, Pisces) → versatile, adaptable, skilled in multiple areas.

**Sreekantha/Mala Yoga**: All benefics in trikonas (1, 5, 9) → extremely auspicious, divine blessings.

---

### 8.8 Important Affliction Combinations

**Graha Yuddha** (Planetary War): When two planets are within 1° of each other (longitude), they fight. The planet with the higher longitude generally loses. Mars and Saturn in Graha Yuddha = intense karmic conflicts.

**Papa Kartari Yoga** (Scissors Yoga by Malefics): A planet or house is hemmed between malefics (Saturn, Mars, Rahu, Ketu, or Sun) on both sides. Suppresses the planet/house.

**Shubha Kartari Yoga** (Scissors Yoga by Benefics): A planet or house is hemmed between benefics (Jupiter, Venus, unafflicted Mercury, waxing Moon). Protects and enhances the planet/house.

**Shakata Yoga**: Jupiter in 6th, 8th, or 12th from Moon → fluctuating fortune, like a wheel of fate; ups and downs throughout life. (Counterpoint: check if Jupiter is still in a kendra from Lagna.)

---

### 8.9 Arishta Yogas (Harmful Combinations)

These create challenges but should be assessed holistically, not as absolute predictions:

1. **Ascendant lord in 8th house**: Health vulnerabilities, identity challenges.
2. **Moon in 8th house, afflicted by malefics**: Emotional instability, possible mental health issues.
3. **Sun and Moon in 8th house**: Identity dissolution, possibly transformative but challenging.
4. **Saturn, Rahu, and Mars in same house (especially 1st, 4th, 7th, or 8th)**: Triple malefic concentration.
5. **12th lord in Lagna, Lagna lord in 12th (Lagna-12th exchange)**: Self-undoing, foreign residence, escapism.

## 9. LORD OF EACH HOUSE ANALYSIS

### 9.1 Lagna Lord Placement

The Lagna lord's house placement tells where life energy flows:
- **Lagna lord in 1st**: Self-reliant, strong health, focused on personal development
- **Lagna lord in 2nd**: Focus on wealth and family, financial orientation
- **Lagna lord in 3rd**: Communication, siblings, self-effort, media
- **Lagna lord in 4th**: Home, mother, property, inner happiness
- **Lagna lord in 5th**: Intelligence, children, creativity, speculation
- **Lagna lord in 6th**: Service, competition, health focus, possible health challenges
- **Lagna lord in 7th**: Partnership-oriented, marriage is central, business partnerships
- **Lagna lord in 8th**: Transformation, occult, longevity focus, health changes
- **Lagna lord in 9th**: Fortune, dharma, father, higher education, travel
- **Lagna lord in 10th**: Career-focused, ambitious, public life, strong professional identity
- **Lagna lord in 11th**: Gains, social networks, elder siblings, wishes fulfilled
- **Lagna lord in 12th**: Spirituality, foreign residence, expenses, liberation

### 9.2 House Lord Exchange (Parivartana Yoga)

When two planets occupy each other's signs, they exchange and create a powerful bond. The houses involved are effectively linked and both are strengthened.

**Great Exchanges** (involving kendra/trikona lords):
- 1st lord ↔ 9th lord: Excellent fortune, dharmic life, fame
- 1st lord ↔ 5th lord: Intelligence, children, creative fortune
- 5th lord ↔ 9th lord: Dual trikona exchange = extraordinary fortune (Lakshmi Yoga)
- 1st lord ↔ 10th lord: Career advancement, famous identity, raj yoga
- 9th lord ↔ 10th lord: Dharma Karma Adhipati Yoga — very powerful raj yoga

**Challenging Exchanges** (involving dusthana lords):
- 1st lord ↔ 6th/8th/12th lord: Challenges to health, life circumstances
- 6th lord ↔ 8th lord: Both dusthana lords together = Viparita yoga possibilities

## 10. SPECIAL LAGNAS AND SENSITIVE POINTS

### 10.1 Arudha Lagna (AL)

The Arudha Lagna (also called Pada Lagna) represents the image or illusion the world perceives about you — your external persona and social standing.

**Calculation**: Count from the Lagna to the Lagna lord's position, then count the same distance again from the Lagna lord. The resulting sign/house is the Arudha Lagna.

**Example**: If Aries rises (Lagna) and Mars (Lagna lord) is in the 7th house (Libra), count 7 from Mars → land in Aries. Since Arudha cannot be in the Lagna or 7th from Lagna, in this case it falls back to the 10th house (Capricorn).

**Rule**: If Arudha falls in the 1st or 7th from Lagna, shift it 10 houses forward.

---

### 10.2 Upapada Lagna (UL)

The Upapada Lagna is the Arudha Pada of the 12th house — it represents the spouse and the quality of marriage in terms of worldly manifestation.

**Calculation**: Count from the 12th house to the 12th lord's position, then count the same distance from the 12th lord.

---

### 10.3 Chandra Lagna (Moon Lagna)

The Moon's sign becomes the 1st house of the Chandra Kundali (Moon chart). This chart is used alongside the natal (birth) chart for a holistic reading. Many classical texts say the Chandra Lagna is as important as the Lagna itself.

---

### 10.4 Navamsha (D-9) — The Fruit of Life

The Navamsha chart is the most important divisional (Varga) chart. It represents:
- The soul's inner nature and spiritual evolution
- Marriage quality and the spouse's nature
- The "ripening" of planetary potential

**Construction**: Each sign (30°) is divided into 9 equal parts (3°20′ each). Each navamsha division corresponds to a sign in sequence.

**Atmakaraka**: The planet with the highest degree (regardless of sign) is the Atmakaraka — the soul significator for this incarnation. Its sign and nakshatra in the Navamsha (Navamsha Lagna = Karakamsha Lagna) shows the soul's evolutionary path.

**Vargottama**: A planet in the same sign in both the natal chart (D-1) and Navamsha (D-9) is Vargottama — extremely strong, as if doubly placed in its own environment.

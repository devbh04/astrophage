## 21. ASTROLOGICAL REMEDIES (UPAYAS)

### 21.1 Philosophy of Remedies

In Vedic astrology, remedies (Upayas) are not about "fixing" a bad chart but about harmonizing with planetary energies through specific actions, intentions, and substances. The philosophy is rooted in karma — every planetary placement reflects past-life karma, and remedies serve as a means to consciously work with that karma rather than passively experiencing its effects.

**Four types of remedies (Chaturvidha Upaya)**:
1. **Mantra** (Sound/Vibration remedies)
2. **Yantra** (Geometric/Visual remedies)
3. **Tantra** (Action/Ritual remedies)
4. **Aushadha** (Herb/Gemstone/Material remedies)

**Principle of remedy selection**: Remedies should strengthen weak benefic planets and pacify strong malefic ones. A debilitated planet that is also a functional benefic for the Lagna should be strengthened. A strong natural malefic (Saturn, Mars, Rahu) that is also a functional malefic should be pacified, not strengthened.

### 21.3 GEMSTONE REMEDIES (RATNA THERAPY)

Gemstones are one of the most powerful and also most risky remedies. They should be worn ONLY after proper astrological consultation.

#### Principle of Gemstone Selection
Gemstones strengthen the planet they represent. Therefore:
- **Wear gemstones for**: Planets that are benefic for the Lagna AND weak/afflicted
- **Do NOT wear gemstones for**: Planets that are malefic for the Lagna (even if they rule good houses in other charts), planets that are strong natural malefics ruling dusthana houses

## 22. DIVISIONAL CHARTS (VARGA/AMSHA CHARTS)

### 22.1 Overview

The Varga charts divide each sign into smaller parts, creating 16 divisional charts (Shodasha Vargas). Each Varga reveals specific areas of life with greater precision than the natal (D-1) chart.

**Why Vargas matter**: A planet in a difficult sign in D-1 may be in its own sign in D-9 (Navamsha), showing that the initial difficulty eventually transforms into strength. Vargas reveal the soul's complete picture.

**Vargottama**: When a planet occupies the same sign in both D-1 and D-9, it is Vargottama — its energy is consistent, concentrated, and extremely powerful (for good or ill depending on the planet's nature).

---

### 22.2 NAVAMSHA (D-9) — The Most Important Varga

**Division**: Each sign (30°) divided into 9 parts of 3°20′ each  
**Rules**: Marriage, spouse, inner self, spiritual path, the fruit of natal planets  

**Construction of Navamsha**:
The Navamsha sign sequence starts from:
- Fire signs (Aries, Leo, Sagittarius): Navamsha starts from Aries
- Earth signs (Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn): Navamsha starts from Capricorn
- Air signs (Gemini, Libra, Aquarius): Navamsha starts from Libra
- Water signs (Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces): Navamsha starts from Cancer

**Key Interpretations**:
- **Navamsha Lagna**: The inner self, spiritual orientation, quality of life in the second half
- **7th house in Navamsha**: Spouse's nature and quality of marriage in depth
- **Atmakaraka in Navamsha**: The soul's purpose (placed in Navamsha Lagna = Karakamsha)
- **Pushkara Navamsha**: Specific degrees in each sign that are supremely auspicious

**Pushkara Navamsha degrees**:
- Aries: 6°40′–10°, 23°20′–26°40′
- Taurus: 3°20′–6°40′, 16°40′–20°
- Gemini: 13°20′–16°40′, 26°40′–30°
- Cancer: 10°–13°20′, 23°20′–26°40′
- Leo: 0°–3°20′, 20°–23°20′
- Virgo: 6°40′–10°, 26°40′–30°
- Libra: 10°–13°20′, 23°20′–26°40′
- Scorpio: 3°20′–6°40′, 16°40′–20°
- Sagittarius: 13°20′–16°40′, 26°40′–30°
- Capricorn: 10°–13°20′, 23°20′–26°40′
- Aquarius: 0°–3°20′, 20°–23°20′
- Pisces: 6°40′–10°, 23°20′–26°40′

A planet in Pushkara Navamsha gains tremendous strength and auspiciousness.

## 23. ASHTAKAVARGA SYSTEM

### 23.1 Overview

Ashtakavarga is a unique Vedic system for evaluating the strength of planetary transits. It assigns points (called Bindus/rekhas) to each sign for each planet based on the natal positions of all 8 planets (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, and Lagna).

**Total possible Bindus**: 8 per sign per planet  
**Average**: 4 Bindus per sign (anything above average is favorable; below is challenging)

### 23.2 Interpreting Ashtakavarga

**For Transit Prediction**:
- When a planet transits a sign with **5 or more Bindus** in its own Ashtakavarga = favorable transit
- When a planet transits a sign with **3 or fewer Bindus** = unfavorable transit
- Saturn with 4+ Bindus in a sign during Sade Sati = Sade Sati is significantly mitigated

**Sarvaashtakavarga** (combined Ashtakavarga):
The sum of all 7 planets' Bindus for each sign.
- 28+ Bindus in a sign = excellent (extremely favorable)
- 25–27 = good
- 22–24 = average
- Below 22 = challenging

**The 8th House Ashtakavarga**: A sign with low Bindus in the 8th house from Lagna = longevity concerns. High Bindus = good longevity.

## 24. SPECIAL VEDIC CONCEPTS

### 24.1 Atmakaraka and Jaimini System

**Atmakaraka** (AK): The planet with the highest degree in the natal chart (regardless of sign). This planet represents the soul's primary signification for this incarnation.

- Sun as AK → Father, authority, ego, pride to be transcended
- Moon as AK → Mother, mind, emotions, care to be developed
- Mars as AK → Courage, siblings, action, aggression to be transcended
- Mercury as AK → Communication, intellect, siblings
- Jupiter as AK → Wisdom, dharma, children, expansion
- Venus as AK → Relationships, love, beauty, material attachment
- Saturn as AK → Discipline, karma, service, renunciation
- Rahu as AK (some authorities) → Foreign elements, unconventional path

**Karakamsha Lagna**: The sign the Atmakaraka occupies in the Navamsha (D-9). Interpreting planets in the Karakamsha and planets aspecting it reveals the soul's evolutionary path and likely spiritual attainments.

### 24.3 Argala (Planetary Intervention)

Argala refers to the intervention or influence of planets placed in certain houses relative to any point. A planet in the 2nd, 4th, or 11th from any house/planet creates Argala (positive intervention). A planet in the 3rd, 12th, or 10th creates Virodhargala (obstruction to the intervention).

Used in Jaimini astrology for predicting events through dasha.

---

### 24.4 Bhava Chalit Chart

The Bhava Chalit chart is constructed by dividing the 360° zodiac into 12 equal houses starting from the exact degree of the Ascendant, not the 0° of the rising sign. This can shift planets into different houses from the whole-sign chart.

**When to use**:
- Traditional Vedic: Primarily uses whole-sign houses for interpretation
- Bhava Chalit: Used for house-cusp analysis and some schools of thought prefer it for determining which house a planet "actually" falls in
- Many astrologers check both: Sign = potential/quality; Bhava = manifestation/events

---

### 24.5 Upapada and the Nature of Marriage

The **Upapada Lagna (UL)** is the Arudha Pada of the 12th house. It shows the manifestation of marriage and the spouse's outer qualities.

**Planets in or aspecting Upapada**:
- Jupiter on UL → wise, benevolent spouse; auspicious marriage
- Venus on UL → beautiful, artistic spouse; romantic marriage
- Saturn on UL → older/serious spouse; delayed marriage; karmic marriage
- Rahu on UL → unconventional/foreign spouse; unusual marriage circumstances
- Ketu on UL → spiritual/detached spouse; marriage may dissolve or be unusual
- Mars on UL (especially without benefic aspect) → conflict in marriage

**2nd from Upapada**:
- Strong benefics here → marriage sustained, family grows from it
- Malefics here → marriage may not last
- Saturn in 2nd from UL → longevity of marriage through duty (Saturn delays but also maintains)
- Ketu in 2nd from UL → possible separation, dissolution of marriage

### 24.6 Graha Yuddha (Planetary War)

When two planets (not luminaries Sun/Moon, and not Rahu/Ketu) come within 1° of each other, they engage in Graha Yuddha (planetary war).

**Rules**:
- The planet with the higher north latitude wins
- If both on same latitude: the one with higher longitude wins
- The loser loses strength and cannot fully deliver its significations

**Effects of specific wars**:
- **Mars-Mercury war**: Confusion between logic and action; accidents
- **Jupiter-Venus war**: Conflict between dharma and desire; teacher-student tensions in life
- **Mars-Jupiter war**: Tension between courage and wisdom; religious arguments
- **Mercury-Venus war**: Communication issues in relationships; artistic/commercial conflicts

---

### 24.7 Combustion (Asta) Detailed

When a planet comes within the combustion range of the Sun, it is "burned" — its significations suffer. However, combust planets carry a hidden quality: they are empowered by the Sun's royal fire and may eventually shine through government or authority channels.

**Planets most frequently combust**: Mercury (always close to Sun), Venus (frequently), Mars (every 2 years for extended periods).

**Combustion effects by planet**:
- Combust Mercury → speech/communication issues, but sharp mind secretly powered by Sun
- Combust Venus → challenges in love/marriage, spouse issues; if Lagna lord combust, health concerns
- Combust Mars → reduced physical courage, brother issues
- Combust Jupiter → wisdom restricted, but solar spiritual wisdom may emerge
- Combust Saturn → very rare; karmic delays intensified

---

### 24.8 Gandanta Degrees — The Junction Points

The Gandanta points are the junctional degrees between water signs (Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces) and fire signs (Aries, Leo, Sagittarius). These 0°–1° areas are considered unstable, karmic, and spiritually charged — like the knot (ganta) between two worlds.

**Three Gandantas**:
1. **Scorpio-Sagittarius (Rashi Gandanta)**: 29° Scorpio – 1° Sagittarius
2. **Pisces-Aries (Rashi Gandanta)**: 29° Pisces – 1° Aries
3. **Cancer-Leo (Rashi Gandanta)**: 29° Cancer – 1° Leo

**Also at Nakshatra Gandanta**:
The last degree of Revati (Pisces), Ashlesha (Cancer), and Jyeshtha (Scorpio) — the final nakshatras of each water sign — are especially karmic.

**Moon in Gandanta**: Considered challenging for the child and possibly for the family. Specific protective ceremonies (Jatakarna, Namakarana) are often recommended.

**Lagna in Gandanta**: Identity and life path may be subject to unusual karmic tests and spiritual depth.

## 26. RELATIONSHIP ASTROLOGY — BEYOND KUNDALI MILAN

### 26.1 Venus and Mars in Synastry

Beyond the Ashtakoota system, examining how one person's Venus and Mars interact with the other's in both charts reveals deep compatibility layers:

**Venus conjunct/trine partner's Moon**: Deep emotional and romantic bond; natural nurturing attraction.

**Mars conjunct/trine partner's Venus**: Strong physical attraction and sexual chemistry.

**Saturn conjunct partner's Moon**: Karmic, long-lasting but potentially emotionally limiting relationship. One partner may feel restricted.

**Jupiter conjunct partner's Venus**: Expansive, generous romantic energy; mutual growth.

**Rahu conjunct partner's Moon**: Intense, karmic, obsessive attraction. Past-life connection.

### 26.2 7th House Analysis for Marriage

**For Men**: Check Venus (natural karaka for wife) — its sign, house, nakshatra, aspects  
**For Women**: Check Jupiter (natural karaka for husband) — its sign, house, nakshatra, aspects  
**For Both**: Check 7th house, 7th lord, and Upapada Lagna

**Timing of marriage**:
- Venus or Jupiter Mahadasha/Antardasha most common
- 7th lord's Mahadasha/Antardasha
- Transit of Jupiter over 7th house, 7th lord, or natal Venus/Jupiter
- Transit of Saturn over 7th often delays but finalizes
